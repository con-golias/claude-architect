## 1. Overview and Scope

ISO/IEC 27001:2022 is the latest version of the international standard for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an Information Security Management System (ISMS). It is published jointly by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) and the International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC).

### Key Changes in the 2022 Edition

- Annex A restructured from 14 domains/114 controls to 4 themes/93 controls.
- 11 new controls added (e.g., threat intelligence, cloud security, data masking).
- Emphasis on process-based approach and integration with other management systems.
- Updated risk assessment terminology aligned with ISO 31000.

### Who Needs ISO 27001

- Organizations handling sensitive customer data.
- Cloud service providers and SaaS companies.
- Organizations in regulated industries (finance, healthcare, government).
- Organizations whose customers require third-party certification of security controls.
- Government suppliers in many jurisdictions.

### Certification vs Compliance

- **Certification**: A third-party certification body audits the ISMS and issues a certificate valid for 3 years (with annual surveillance audits).
- **Compliance**: An organization follows the standard's requirements without formal certification. Compliance alone does not allow claiming certification.

## 2. Information Security Management System (ISMS)

### ISMS Definition

An ISMS is a systematic approach to managing sensitive information so that it remains secure. It encompasses people, processes, and technology.

### ISMS Scope

Define the boundaries of the ISMS including:
- Organizational units and locations.
- Information systems and infrastructure.
- Business processes and services.
- Third-party relationships.

```yaml
isms_scope_example:
  organization: "Acme SaaS Platform"
  scope: "The information security management system covering the design, development, operation, and support of the Acme SaaS platform and its supporting infrastructure"
  locations:
    - "Primary office: Berlin, Germany"
    - "Cloud infrastructure: AWS eu-west-1, eu-central-1"
  systems:
    - "Production application infrastructure"
    - "Development and CI/CD systems"
    - "Corporate IT systems"
    - "Customer support systems"
  exclusions:
    - "Marketing website (static, no customer data)"
  justification_for_exclusions: "Marketing website does not process, store, or transmit information within scope of the ISMS"
```

## 3. Plan-Do-Check-Act Cycle

ISO 27001 follows the PDCA cycle for continuous improvement of the ISMS.

### Plan

- Establish the ISMS policy, objectives, processes, and procedures.
- Conduct risk assessment and risk treatment.
- Define the Statement of Applicability.
- Develop the risk treatment plan.

### Do

- Implement the risk treatment plan.
- Implement the Annex A controls selected in the SoA.
- Train and raise awareness among personnel.
- Operate the ISMS processes.

### Check

- Monitor and measure the performance of the ISMS.
- Conduct internal audits.
- Perform management reviews.
- Review the effectiveness of controls.

### Act

- Address nonconformities and take corrective action.
- Continually improve the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the ISMS.
- Update risk assessments and treatment plans as needed.

```yaml
pdca_developer_mapping:
  plan:
    - "Participate in threat modeling for new systems"
    - "Identify security requirements for features"
    - "Define security architecture for new services"

  do:
    - "Implement security controls in code"
    - "Configure infrastructure securely"
    - "Participate in security training"
    - "Follow secure development practices"

  check:
    - "Review security scan results"
    - "Participate in internal audits"
    - "Review monitoring dashboards and alerts"
    - "Conduct code security reviews"

  act:
    - "Remediate vulnerabilities identified in scans"
    - "Address audit findings"
    - "Update security practices based on incidents"
    - "Improve automation of security controls"
```

## 4. Risk Assessment and Treatment

### Risk Assessment Process (Clause 6.1.2)

```yaml
risk_assessment_process:
  step_1_establish_context:
    - "Define risk assessment criteria (likelihood, impact)"
    - "Define risk acceptance criteria"
    - "Identify the scope of the assessment"

  step_2_identify_risks:
    - "Identify information assets"
    - "Identify threats to those assets"
    - "Identify vulnerabilities that threats could exploit"
    - "Identify existing controls"

  step_3_analyze_risks:
    - "Assess the likelihood of each risk materializing"
    - "Assess the impact if the risk materializes"
    - "Determine the level of risk (likelihood x impact)"

  step_4_evaluate_risks:
    - "Compare risk levels against risk acceptance criteria"
    - "Prioritize risks for treatment"

  risk_scales:
    likelihood:
      1: "Rare - unlikely to occur"
      2: "Unlikely - could occur but not expected"
      3: "Possible - may occur"
      4: "Likely - expected to occur"
      5: "Almost certain - will occur"

    impact:
      1: "Negligible - minimal impact"
      2: "Minor - limited impact"
      3: "Moderate - significant impact"
      4: "Major - severe impact"
      5: "Critical - catastrophic impact"

    risk_level:
      1_to_4: "Low - accept"
      5_to_9: "Medium - consider treatment"
      10_to_16: "High - treat"
      17_to_25: "Critical - immediate treatment required"
```

### Risk Treatment Options (Clause 6.1.3)

```yaml
risk_treatment_options:
  modify:
    description: "Implement controls to reduce the risk"
    example: "Implement MFA to reduce the risk of unauthorized access"

  retain:
    description: "Accept the risk as it is within risk appetite"
    example: "Accept the risk of a brief service interruption during maintenance windows"

  avoid:
    description: "Remove the activity or condition that causes the risk"
    example: "Stop collecting unnecessary personal data"

  share:
    description: "Transfer or share the risk with a third party"
    example: "Purchase cyber insurance; use a managed security service"
```

## 5. Statement of Applicability (SoA)

The Statement of Applicability is a required document that lists all Annex A controls and states:
- Whether each control is applicable or not.
- Justification for inclusion or exclusion.
- Implementation status.

```yaml
soa_example:
  - control_id: "A.8.5"
    control_name: "Secure authentication"
    applicable: true
    justification: "All systems require secure authentication to protect information assets"
    implementation_status: "implemented"
    implementation_details: "SSO via Okta with FIDO2 MFA enforced for all users"

  - control_id: "A.7.4"
    control_name: "Physical security monitoring"
    applicable: false
    justification: "Organization is fully remote with no physical office; all infrastructure is cloud-based"
    implementation_status: "not_applicable"
    implementation_details: "Cloud provider (AWS) maintains physical security controls (covered under shared responsibility)"

  - control_id: "A.8.28"
    control_name: "Secure coding"
    applicable: true
    justification: "Organization develops custom software that processes sensitive customer data"
    implementation_status: "implemented"
    implementation_details: "SAST in CI pipeline, mandatory code review, annual secure coding training"
```

### A.8.2 Privileged Access Rights

```yaml
  name: "Privileged access rights"
  description: "Restrict and manage privileged access"
  implementation:
    - "Maintain an inventory of privileged accounts"
    - "Use separate accounts for administrative access"

```

```python
# Just-in-time privileged access example
class JITAccessManager:
    """Grant temporary elevated privileges with audit logging."""

    MAX_DURATION_HOURS = 4

    async def request_elevated_access(
        self,
        user_id: str,
        role: str,
        justification: str,
        duration_hours: float,
    ) -> AccessGrant:
        if duration_hours > self.MAX_DURATION_HOURS:
            raise ValueError(f"Maximum duration is {self.MAX_DURATION_HOURS} hours")

        # Require approval for sensitive roles
        if role in self.roles_requiring_approval:
            approval = await self.get_manager_approval(user_id, role, justification)
            if not approval.granted:
                raise AccessDeniedError("Manager approval denied")

        # Grant temporary access
        grant = AccessGrant(
            user_id=user_id,
            role=role,
            granted_at=datetime.utcnow(),
            expires_at=datetime.utcnow() + timedelta(hours=duration_hours),
            justification=justification,
        )

        await self.iam.grant_temporary_role(user_id, role, grant.expires_at)
        await self.audit_log.record("PRIVILEGED_ACCESS_GRANTED", grant.__dict__)

        # Schedule automatic revocation
        await self.scheduler.schedule(
            at=grant.expires_at,
            action=self.revoke_access,
            args=(grant.id,),
        )

        return grant

    async def revoke_access(self, grant_id: str):
        grant = await self.store.get(grant_id)
        await self.iam.revoke_role(grant.user_id, grant.role)
        await self.audit_log.record("PRIVILEGED_ACCESS_REVOKED", {"grant_id": grant_id})
```

## 14. Code and Configuration Examples

### Automated Configuration Compliance Checking

```python
"""
Automated ISO 27001 Annex A.8.9 configuration compliance checker.
Verifies infrastructure configuration against security baselines.
"""

class ConfigurationComplianceChecker:
    def __init__(self, cloud_client, baseline: dict):
        self.cloud = cloud_client
        self.baseline = baseline

    async def check_all(self) -> ComplianceReport:
        report = ComplianceReport()

        # A.8.24: Encryption at rest
        report.add_results(await self.check_encryption_at_rest())

        # A.8.5: Secure authentication
        report.add_results(await self.check_mfa_enforcement())

        # A.8.3: Information access restriction
        report.add_results(await self.check_public_access())

        # A.8.16: Monitoring
        report.add_results(await self.check_logging_enabled())

        return report

    async def check_encryption_at_rest(self) -> list[ComplianceResult]:
        results = []
        databases = await self.cloud.list_databases()
        for db in databases:
            encrypted = await self.cloud.is_encrypted(db.id)
            results.append(ComplianceResult(
                control="A.8.24",
                resource=db.id,
                compliant=encrypted,
                finding=None if encrypted else f"Database {db.name} is not encrypted at rest",
            ))
        return results

    async def check_mfa_enforcement(self) -> list[ComplianceResult]:
        results = []
        users = await self.cloud.list_iam_users()
        for user in users:
            mfa_enabled = await self.cloud.has_mfa(user.id)
            results.append(ComplianceResult(
                control="A.8.5",
                resource=f"user/{user.name}",
                compliant=mfa_enabled,
                finding=None if mfa_enabled else f"User {user.name} does not have MFA enabled",
            ))
        return results

    async def check_public_access(self) -> list[ComplianceResult]:
        results = []
        buckets = await self.cloud.list_storage_buckets()
        for bucket in buckets:
            public = await self.cloud.is_public(bucket.id)
            results.append(ComplianceResult(
                control="A.8.3",
                resource=bucket.id,
                compliant=not public,
                finding=None if not public else f"Bucket {bucket.name} is publicly accessible",
            ))
        return results
```

### Security Event Reporting (A.6.8)

```typescript
// Security event reporting system
interface SecurityEvent {
  id: string;
  reportedBy: string;
  reportedAt: string;
  eventType: 'vulnerability' | 'policy_violation' | 'suspicious_activity' | 'data_exposure' | 'other';
  severity: 'low' | 'medium' | 'high' | 'critical';
  description: string;
  affectedSystems: string[];
  immediateActions: string[];
  status: 'reported' | 'investigating' | 'resolved' | 'closed';
}

async function reportSecurityEvent(event: Omit<SecurityEvent, 'id' | 'reportedAt' | 'status'>): Promise<SecurityEvent> {
  const securityEvent: SecurityEvent = {
    ...event,
    id: generateId(),
    reportedAt: new Date().toISOString(),
    status: 'reported',
  };

  // Store the event
  await securityEventStore.save(securityEvent);

  // Notify security team based on severity
  if (securityEvent.severity === 'critical' || securityEvent.severity === 'high') {
    await alerting.sendImmediate('security-team', securityEvent);
  } else {
    await alerting.sendStandard('security-team', securityEvent);
  }

  // Create a tracking ticket
  await ticketSystem.create({
    title: `Security Event: ${securityEvent.eventType} - ${securityEvent.severity}`,
    description: securityEvent.description,
    assignee: 'security-team',
    priority: securityEvent.severity,
    labels: ['security-event', `severity-${securityEvent.severity}`],
  });

  return securityEvent;
}
```

## 15. Best Practices

1. **Treat the ISMS as a living management system, not a documentation exercise.** The ISMS must actively govern information security decisions. Policies and procedures that exist only on paper fail audits and fail to protect information.

2. **Base control selection on risk assessment, not checkbox compliance.** Select Annex A controls based on the risks identified in your assessment. Document why each control is or is not applicable in the Statement of Applicability.

3. **Embed security into the software development lifecycle (A.8.25, A.8.28).** Integrate SAST, DAST, and dependency scanning into CI/CD pipelines. Require security-focused code review. Train developers in secure coding annually.

4. **Implement Infrastructure as Code for configuration management (A.8.9).** Define all infrastructure in version-controlled code. Enforce review processes for infrastructure changes. Use drift detection to identify unauthorized modifications.

5. **Separate development, testing, and production environments completely (A.8.31).** Use separate cloud accounts, credentials, and network segments for each environment. Never use production data in non-production environments.

6. **Implement just-in-time privileged access instead of standing privileges (A.8.2).** Grant elevated permissions only when needed, for a limited time, with full audit logging. Review and revoke privileged access automatically.

7. **Conduct internal audits focused on effectiveness, not just existence.** Internal audits should verify that controls are working as intended, not just that documents exist. Test controls by sampling real evidence of operation.

8. **Maintain a comprehensive risk register and review it at least quarterly.** The risk register should be a living document that reflects current threats, vulnerabilities, and risk levels. Update it whenever significant changes occur.

9. **Automate compliance monitoring and evidence collection.** Use tools (Vanta, Drata, AWS Config, Azure Policy) to continuously monitor compliance posture. Automated evidence reduces audit preparation effort.

10. **Use the management review process to drive actual improvement.** Management reviews should result in actionable decisions: resource allocation, process changes, risk acceptance. Document decisions and track follow-through.

## 16. Anti-Patterns

1. **Treating ISO 27001 as an IT-only initiative.** ISO 27001 is a management system standard that requires organizational commitment. Without management support and cross-functional involvement, the ISMS will lack authority and resources.

2. **Copying policies from templates without customization.** Generic policies that do not reflect actual organizational practices create gaps between documentation and reality. Auditors test whether policies match practice.

3. **Performing risk assessment as a one-time activity.** Risk assessment must be a continuous process, updated when the threat landscape, systems, or business context changes. Annual risk assessment is the minimum; event-driven updates are essential.

4. **Selecting all Annex A controls without risk-based justification.** Not all 93 controls will be applicable to every organization. Selecting controls without risk assessment undermines the risk-based approach that ISO 27001 requires.

5. **Ignoring the continuous improvement cycle.** Certification is not the end goal. Organizations that stop improving after certification typically face findings in surveillance audits and degrade their security posture.

6. **Maintaining separate "audit" versions of documents.** If the documents shown to auditors differ from the documents used by the team, the ISMS is not effective. Maintain a single source of truth for all policies and procedures.

7. **Not conducting internal audits with sufficient independence.** Internal auditors should not audit their own work. Ensure auditors are independent of the processes they audit, or use external parties for internal audits.

8. **Failing to integrate security into project management (A.5.8).** Security requirements should be part of every project from the beginning, not added as an afterthought before launch.

## 17. Enforcement Checklist

### ISMS Governance
- [ ] ISMS scope is defined and documented.
- [ ] Information security policy is approved by management.
- [ ] Information security objectives are defined and measurable.
- [ ] Roles and responsibilities for information security are assigned.
- [ ] Management reviews are conducted at least annually.
- [ ] Internal audits are conducted at least annually.

### Risk Management
- [ ] Risk assessment methodology is documented.
- [ ] Risk assessment covers all information assets in scope.
- [ ] Risk register is maintained and reviewed quarterly.
- [ ] Risk treatment plans are documented with ownership and timelines.
- [ ] Statement of Applicability is complete and current.
- [ ] Residual risks are formally accepted by management.

### Access Control (A.5.15, A.8.2, A.8.3, A.8.5)
- [ ] Access control policy is documented and enforced.
- [ ] All users have unique identifiers.
- [ ] MFA is enforced for all system access.
- [ ] Privileged access is restricted, logged, and reviewed quarterly.
- [ ] Access is revoked within 24 hours of role change or termination.
- [ ] Quarterly access reviews are conducted and documented.

### Secure Development (A.8.25, A.8.26, A.8.27, A.8.28)
- [ ] Secure development lifecycle is documented and followed.
- [ ] Security requirements are included in feature specifications.
- [ ] SAST is integrated into the CI pipeline.
- [ ] Dependency scanning runs on every build.
- [ ] Code review is required before merge to production branch.
- [ ] Annual secure coding training is completed by all developers.
- [ ] Threat modeling is performed for new systems and significant changes.

### Configuration Management (A.8.9)
- [ ] Security configuration baselines are defined.
- [ ] Infrastructure is managed as code.
- [ ] Configuration drift detection is operational.
- [ ] Configuration changes are reviewed and approved.
- [ ] CIS Benchmarks or equivalent are applied.

### Cryptography (A.8.24)
- [ ] Cryptographic policy defines approved algorithms and key lengths.
- [ ] All data at rest is encrypted (AES-256 or equivalent).
- [ ] All data in transit is encrypted (TLS 1.2+).
- [ ] Key management procedures are documented.
- [ ] Key rotation is automated with defined periods.
- [ ] Deprecated algorithms are not in use.

### Monitoring and Logging (A.8.16)
- [ ] Centralized log aggregation is operational.
- [ ] Security events are monitored in real time.
- [ ] Automated alerting is configured for anomalies.
- [ ] Log retention meets policy requirements.
- [ ] Log integrity is protected (tamper-evident storage).

### Incident Management (A.5.26)
- [ ] Incident response plan is documented and tested.
- [ ] Security events can be reported through defined channels.
- [ ] Incidents are tracked to resolution.
- [ ] Post-incident reviews are conducted.
- [ ] Lessons learned are incorporated into improvements.

### Business Continuity (A.5.29, A.5.30)
- [ ] Business continuity plans are documented.
- [ ] Disaster recovery procedures are tested at least annually.
- [ ] Backup procedures are automated and tested.
- [ ] Recovery time and recovery point objectives are defined and met.

### Environment Separation (A.8.31, A.8.33)
- [ ] Development, testing, and production environments are separated.
- [ ] Separate accounts/credentials for each environment.
- [ ] Production data is not used in non-production environments.
- [ ] Test data is appropriately managed and protected.

### People Security (A.6)
- [ ] Background checks are performed for personnel with access to sensitive systems.
- [ ] Security responsibilities are included in employment agreements.
- [ ] Annual security awareness training is completed by all personnel.
- [ ] Security event reporting procedures are communicated to all personnel.
- [ ] Offboarding procedures include immediate access revocation.
